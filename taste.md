# SIGOUT taste profile

This file is how the curator decides what goes on sigout. It reads this before
judging every pending event. **Edit it freely in plain English** — the next
run obeys whatever it says here. When an auto-decision annoys you, fix the
rule that produced it, not just the event.

## What sigout is

A curated, no-fluff list of things genuinely worth leaving the house for in
Singapore. The taste behind it loves: independent art, craft with character,
small-press and literary community, serious museum shows, wellness with soul,
indie markets, and food with a story. Discerning — a big name or a big
marketing budget is never by itself a reason to publish.

## Hard no — reject on sight

- **Arena/stadium headliner concerts and hard techno or rave club nights**
  (Eric Prydz, 999999999, Ministry of Rave energy). Fame is not a fit.
- **Kids / family / parenting** anything: camps, toddler programmes,
  school-holiday activities, parenting workshops.
- **Auctions and collector sales**: Hotlotz salerooms, coins & banknotes,
  vintage watches, jewellery lots, wine & whisky collections.
- **Corporate and brand promos dressed as events**: mall activations,
  restaurant anniversaries and menu collabs, product launches, branded craft
  booths (the BreadTalk-keychain genre), F1 promo machinery.
- **Immersive commercial spectacles**: Disney experiences, Van Gogh-style
  projection rooms, cosplay expo days. Exception (Jane's call on Hockney's
  Bigger & Closer): an immersive show authored by a major artist about their
  own work is in; branded theme spectacles stay out.
- **Mall pushcart maker markets** — the weekly INVADE-style circuits at PLQ /
  Dhoby Ghaut. (Curated indie markets are a strong yes — see below. The
  difference is a point of view, not a venue.)
- **Races, runs, sports fiestas.** Generic hobbies and consumer fairs.
- Things that aren't events: gallery opening hours, standing services
  (massages), venue ads.

## Strong yes

- **The serious institutions** — National Gallery, SAM, ACM, STPI, Esplanade:
  headline exhibitions and festivals. Their attached programming (artist
  talks, participatory corners, hands-on classes) is also in when the show
  itself makes the cut — but **plain guided tours are out**, even of
  published shows (Jane's call on the Zarina and ACM tours).
- **Independent galleries** — Mizuma, Cuturi, artcommune, Gallery 1819,
  ART SEASONS, Art Again and friends: real shows by real artists.
- **The indie community rooms** — Casual Poet Library, Stranger
  Conversations / Strangerconvos, The Living Room, Arterly Obsessed, Cityluxe,
  anything at 195 Pearl's Hill Terrace: book clubs, writing sessions,
  journaling, supper-club talks, life drawing, poetry nights, zine circles.
- **Craft with character** — STPI printmaking classes, batik, pottery,
  florals, beading, natural dyes. Sound baths and gentle wellness (crystal
  bowls, meditation, breathwork) with a real practitioner.
- **Indie / curated markets** — Me-You Market, Craft Fest SG, zine fairs,
  vintage flea, design markets.
- **Food with a story** — heritage or food-culture pop-ups (Pasarfish),
  characterful food festivals (oyster festival, GastroBeats genre). Not
  restaurant marketing.
- **Community with charm** — silent reading clubs, fountain-pen meets,
  pet-friendly parties at interesting venues (New Bahru), conscious-dating
  socials by the indie rooms above. We Read Together's per-café meetups are
  **listed individually** (Jane's call) — don't fold them into one umbrella
  card; when a session's scraped date has passed, use the next occurrence.
- **Arts festivals** — SIFA, HeritageFest, i Light, Singapore Gallery Month,
  community culture weekends.

## Music bar

**Vibe over names.** Beach-club day parties, silent disco, indie club nights
(Malu Malu), art-music evenings, intimate concerts (a piano series in a small
room). Skip arena headliners even when the act is beloved — Massive Attack
was skipped under this rule — and skip hard techno / rave bills.

## Posture

**Generous.** Publish when it plausibly fits (roughly 70%+ confident); Jane
removes misses via /curate in seconds. Use **unsure** only when genuinely
torn, or when the information is too thin to write an honest card.

Mechanical rules the curator must always follow:

- An event with **no date and no end date** cannot be placed on the site —
  leave it pending (unsure) with a note, never publish it.
- **Never touch events already published** — Jane's edits always win.
- Duplicates of the same event (scraper variants) are collapsed to the
  fullest copy; the rest are dropped silently as duplicates, not rejections.
- If it smells like thin-info promo → reject. If it's taste-plausible but
  missing a date or venue → unsure.
